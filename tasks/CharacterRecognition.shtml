Instructions

This task consists of many subtasks. In each subtask, you will be shown an image of a hand-drawn character for a fraction of a second, after which it will disappear. Then you will be shown an image of another hand-drawn charater in the right box. You must decide whether or not these images are drawings of the same character.

More precisely, each subtask consists of the following:

  1. A red border will flash once around the box that will contain the first image.
  2. About half a second later, the first image will appear.
  3. Less than half a second later, the image will disappear.
  4. About a second later, the second image will appear in the right box, and will not disappear until you answer the question.
  5. Every few characters, the progress bar on the bottom will be updated to show how many you've gotten right, and how many you've gotten wrong.

Please note:
  • You must complete every subtask to submit the form. (The questions at the end, however, are optional.)
    • If you somehow manage to submit the form without completing every subtask, your submission will be rejected.
  • So long as you complete every subtask, and you make an effort to answer the questions correctly, your submission will be accepted regardless of how many you get correct.

Alternate Instructions (Brenden)

This experiment tests your ability to discriminate handwritten characters from writing systems around the world. For example in the Latin alphabet A, B, C, and D are different characters. You will see pairs of character images, and no two images are exactly the same. If you think the two images contain the same character (e.g., two different people writing the letter B), then mark "Same." If they contain different characters (B and D), mark "Different." The exact characters you will see should be new to you, so make your best guess.

You will see a sequence of 200 trials each requiring a response. During a trial, a red square appears in the left box "Example Image" to cue your attention. Shortly after the cue, an example character is quickly flashed in that box. After the flash, it is masked by a nonsense image. Then a test character appears in the "Test Image" box to the right. Your job is to indicate whether the example character and test character are the same or different.
