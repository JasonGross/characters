Instructions

You must use the Mozilla Firefox web browser.

This HIT consists of many subtasks, and you must complete every subtask to submit the form.

This task tests your ability to draw characters from writing systems around the world. You will be shown an image of a character, such as for a fraction of a second, after which it will be replaced by noise. You will then be asked to draw the character you have seen.

You will see a sequence of 200 subtasks each requiring a response. Each subtask has the following structure:

  1. A red square appears in the left box "Example Image" to cue your attention.
  2. Shortly after the cue, an example character is quickly flashed in that red square.
  3. After the flash, the character disappears and is replaced by a nonsense scribble image.
  4. Shortly after the cue, an example character appears in that red square.
  5. A box labeled "Drawing" then appears, in which you can draw what you think the character looked like.

Please note:

  • You must complete every subtask to submit the form. (The questions at the end, however, are optional.)
    • If you submit the task with scribbles or single dots in the boxes, your submission will be rejected.
  • So long as you complete every subtask, and you make an effort to draw the characters accurately, your submission will be accepted regardless of how accurate you are.
